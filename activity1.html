Activity 1: Hybrid Renewable Energy System (Solar + Wind)

Explanation

The hybrid solarâ€“wind system ensures a continuous power supply to a rural health clinic by combining two renewable energy sources. Solar photovoltaic panels convert sunlight into DC electricity during the day, while a small wind turbine captures kinetic wind energy and produces AC power. Both energy sources feed into a charge controller that manages charging of a common battery bank, preventing overcharging and deep discharge. The inverter converts stored DC power into AC for clinic equipment, lighting, and cold storage. During calm or cloudy weather, the hybrid nature ensures that one source compensates for the other, guaranteeing reliability. Surplus energy can be exported to the local grid. This system is sustainable, cost-effective, and vital for ensuring uninterrupted medical services in off-grid regions.
