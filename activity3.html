Activity 3: Biomass-based Biogas Generation for Dairy Farm

Explanation

The biogas system converts dairy waste from 100 cattle into clean energy through anaerobic digestion. Cattle dung and water are mixed in a slurry tank and fed into a sealed digester. Microbial action decomposes the organic matter, releasing methane-rich biogas. This gas is collected in a gas holder and supplied to a dual-fuel generator or used directly for cooking and heating. The remaining digestate serves as organic fertilizer for crops. By converting waste into energy, this system minimizes greenhouse emissions, reduces odor, and provides energy self-reliance for rural dairy farms, supporting sustainable agriculture and circular economy principles.
